# Linene Graywind — Agent of the Lionshield Coster

_Tough, principled human woman. Runs the [[Lionshield Coster]] trading post in [[Phandalin]] on behalf of the [[Lionshield Coster|Lionshield]] merchant company out of [[Yartar]]._

---

## At a Glance

- **Profession:** Independent merchant agent. Sells weapons, armor, and specialized adventuring gear.
- **Where to find her:** Behind the counter at the [[Lionshield Coster]] on the north side of [[Phandalin]]'s town square.
- **Disposition:** Direct. Skeptical of strangers. Warm to anyone who has clearly done the work.

---

## Personality

Linene is not interested in being your friend. She is interested in being your reliable supplier. She speaks plainly, never overcharges, never gives discounts, and will throw you out of her shop if she suspects you are working with the wrong people. The [[The Redbrands|Redbrands]] have been told — twice — that she will sell them nothing. They have not pressed the point.

---

## What She Wants

The party has _already won her loyalty_ by recovering the **Lionshield caravan goods** Klarg's bugbears stole.

- She paid the **50gp reward** when they delivered the goods.
- She will sell them anything in the [[adventuring-gear-phb|adventuring gear table]] at standard PHB prices.
- She will _quietly_ set aside a piece or two of weapons or armor she thinks one of them needs, at fair price.
- She despises the Redbrands and will share whatever she knows about them. She is not a fighter, but she has friends in [[Neverwinter]] who are.

---

## What She Knows

- The Redbrands have been raiding Lionshield caravans on the [[Triboar Trail]] for months. She does not know if Glasstaff is personally directing it, but she knows the gang is being supplied with weapons she did not sell them.
- She has seen one of the Redbrands' lieutenants, a man named **Hyrik** — heavyset, scarred across the forehead, walks with a limp — coming and going from the east end of town. Probably from [[Tresendar Manor]].
- [[Sildar Hallwinter]] is "the only honest man with a badge for a hundred miles."
