# Session 4: Blood in the Square

**Played:** Session 4
**Setting:** [[Phandalin]]

---

## Recap

### Settling Accounts

The party arrived in [[Phandalin]] with the captured Lionshield wagon and a battered [[Sildar Hallwinter]]. First stop was [[Barthen's Provisions]], where **Elmar Barthen** received [[Gundren Rockseeker|Gundren]]'s long-overdue mining supplies and paid the party **10gp each** as promised — plus a handful of extra healing supplies thrown in on the house.

Across town, [[Linene Graywind]] at the [[Lionshield Coster]] took delivery of her recovered crates and paid the **50gp reward**. The party used the windfall to restock and pick up a few proper weapons from her back room.

Somewhere in there — and somewhere quiet — the party also took a long enough moment with the small jeweled newt figurine recovered from [[Cragmaw Hideout]] to learn what it actually was: a [[figurine-of-wondrous-power|Figurine of Wondrous Power]]. The party named it **Newton**.

### Trouble at the Shrine of Luck

The peace did not last. Outside the [[Shrine of Luck]], the party walked into four [[The Redbrands|Redbrand]] thugs — drunk, loud, and amusing themselves by intimidating townspeople on the temple steps. Their leader was a heavyset ruffian called **Graul**.

The party tried diplomacy first. A **Charm** attempt on Graul slid right off him — he was too belligerent, too drunk, and too sure of his cloak. When negotiation collapsed, the party committed.

**Versace** shifted into a **brown bear**. **Orla** dropped into a **wolf**. **Grom** and **Fleck** held the line in front of the shrine. The fight was loud, brief, and bloody. All four Redbrands died on the temple steps.

Watching the whole thing from across the road: **[[Carp Alderleaf]]**, a 10-year-old halfling boy with a wooden sword, eyes the size of dinner plates. When the fighting was over, Carp flipped one of the party a copper coin as a tip — apparently his entire savings — and ran off home before his mother could find out where he'd been.

### Sildar's Writ

[[Sildar Hallwinter|Sildar]] arrived in the immediate aftermath, sword still in its scabbard but his face very serious. To keep the killings from sliding into "barroom murder" in the townmaster's eyes, he formally hired the party on behalf of the [[The Lords' Alliance|Lords' Alliance]] — making the killings sanctioned acts by Alliance contractors.

The party was then walked to the [[Townmaster's Hall]] and sworn in as **Phandalin protectorate** by [[Harbin Wester|Townmaster Wester]]. **20gp each** for the official appointment. The party now has legal authority to maintain order in [[Phandalin]] — and the formal backing of the [[The Lords' Alliance|Lords' Alliance]] to do it.

### Sildar's Three Asks

Back at the [[Stonehill Inn]], Sildar laid out his priorities:

1. **Find [[Gundren Rockseeker]]** — last seen being dragged toward a place called [[Cragmaw Castle]], somewhere in the [[Neverwinter Wood]].
2. **Find [[Iarno Albrek]]** — a [[The Lords' Alliance|Lords' Alliance]] wizard sent to Phandalin a year ago and never heard from since.
3. **Eliminate the Redbrand threat in town** — bounty of **200gp**, payable from Alliance funds.

He also let the party know about two missing civilians the Redbrands appear to have taken: **Mirna** (the wife) and **Nars** (the son) of the murdered woodcarver [[Thel Dendrar]].

### A Question for a Banshee

[[Sister Garaele]] approached the party at the [[Shrine of Luck]] with her own request: a journey to [[Conyberry]] to put a single question to the banshee **[[Agatha]]** — *"Where is the spellbook of Bowgentle?"*

Her offer: **25gp each**, a silver comb to present as a gift, and a handful of Tymora coins for luck on the road. Her warning: Agatha must be addressed as a noble lady, treated with formality, and asked _one_ question only. Get it wrong, and the audience ends in a way that does not include the party walking back to Phandalin.

### Reconnaissance

Before the night was over, the party took stock of their targets:

- **[[Sleeping Giant]]** — scouted via a small animal sent ahead. Eight occupants total: seven Redbrands and a single bartender. Five Redbrands were downstairs nursing hangovers; three were upstairs gossiping about the previous night's events. Two exits: the front door and a kitchen door at the back. Several broken windows from the gang's rowdiness.
- **The tunnel in the woods** — [[Carp Alderleaf|Carp]] led the party to the brambled boulder where he'd found his "secret tunnel." Two Redbrands were posted as guards. Carp nearly gave the party's position away while gawking. The party pulled back successfully without being spotted.

## What the Party Walks Away With

- **10gp each** from Barthen for the Gundren delivery
- **50gp** from [[Linene Graywind|Linene]] for the recovered Lionshield cargo
- **20gp each** from the townmaster for the formal protectorate appointment
- An official **Lords' Alliance writ of commission** signed by [[Sildar Hallwinter|Sildar]]
- The newt figurine — now identified as a **[[figurine-of-wondrous-power|Figurine of Wondrous Power]]**, named **Newton**
- A silver comb and a handful of Tymora coins (from [[Sister Garaele]])
- The respect (and apprehension) of [[Phandalin]]: four dead Redbrands and a lot of witnesses
- Solid intel on the [[Sleeping Giant]] (8 occupants, 2 exits) and the [[Tresendar Manor]] back tunnel (2 guards)
